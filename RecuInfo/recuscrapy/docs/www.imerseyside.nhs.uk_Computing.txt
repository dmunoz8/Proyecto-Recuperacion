     <h1>
      Green Computing
     </h1>
     <p>
      The NHS has pledged to 'go green' and significantly reduce its carbon footprint in line with government targets to achieve an 80% reduction in carbon emissions by 2050.
     </p>
     <p>
      Many NHS Trusts are responding to this objective by taking active measures to reduce their carbon footprint by enthusiastically promoting carbon awareness at every level of the organisation. Such culture change can take time and this is why Informatics Merseyside has been exploring new tools and techniques, which will help deliver quick wins in the battle to reduce carbon emissions and achieve cost savings.
     </p>
     <p>
      One such tool, which is proving particularly popular, is PowerStudio by Cererto. Used by several trusts in the Merseyside Health Economy, the software reduces energy consumption and prolongs the life of computer equipment by powering down unused PCs, in a way that does not impact or frustrate the PC user. Significant savings can be measured as cost, CO2 emissions and kWh and it is estimated that a return on investment can be achieved within 5 months.
     </p>
     <p>
      There are also other initiatives such as Cloud Computing, Mobile Working and Video Collaboration which are helping to reduce running costs and support the green agenda through reductions in carbon emissions.
     </p>
     <p>
      In addition to advising on useful tools and techniques, Informatics Merseyside proactively keeps abreast of green and government initiatives to reduce the CO2 emissions of its partner organisations.
     </p>
