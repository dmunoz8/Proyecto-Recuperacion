            <p>
             I could not find any difference... or at least no good source that definitely distinguishes both.
            </p>
            <p>
             I tried:
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              Wikipedia (
              <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Informatics" rel="nofollow">
               Informatics
              </a>
              has a redirect to Computer Science), so I guess the only difference is the context in which both are used
             </li>
             <li>
              Google:
              <ul>
               <li>
                <a href="http://www.informatics.uci.edu/qa/index.php#ugrad02" rel="nofollow">
                 University of California
                </a>
                :
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  Computer Science: internal features, structure and behavior of computer systems
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Informatics: relationship between what is inside the computer and what is outside (What does this mean? I study ... well, I thought it would be called "Computer Science", but I'm not sure now. )
                 </li>
                </ul>
               </li>
               <li>
                <a href="http://forum.wordreference.com/showthread.php?t=7587&amp;s=4f1c643670a344a153fccf5bbca5ec38&amp;p=53232#post53232" rel="nofollow">
                 forum.wordreference.com
                </a>
                :
                <ul>
                 <li>
                  Computer Science: software engineering, algorithms
                 </li>
                 <li>
                  Informatics: installing new software, upgrades,networks LAN WANS, firewalls
                 </li>
                </ul>
               </li>
              </ul>
             </li>
             <li>
              <a href="http://dict.leo.org/#/search=informatik&amp;searchLoc=0&amp;resultOrder=basic&amp;multiwordShowSingle=on" rel="nofollow">
               dict.leo.org
              </a>
              : Both are translated with "Informatik" in German.
             </li>
             <li>
              English.StackExchange.com: No result
             </li>
            </ul>

           <div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
            <p>
             In the link to
             <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Informatics" rel="nofollow">
              Wikipedia
             </a>
             that you provide, the very first sublisting gives a definition of
             <strong>
              informatics
             </strong>
            </p>
            <blockquote>
             <p>
              a broad academic field encompassing computer science, human-computer interaction, information science, information technology, algorithms, areas of mathematics (especially mathematical logic and category theory), and social sciences that are involved
             </p>
            </blockquote>
            <p>
             This definition clearly includes
             <em>
              computer science
             </em>
             as a subset of
             <em>
              informatics
             </em>
             , but goes on to list other topics that are beyond the scope of pure
             <em>
              computer science
             </em>
             , such as
             <em>
              social sciences that are involved
             </em>
             .
            </p>
            <p>
             While a full degree program in
             <em>
              computer science
             </em>
             might require a student to take industrial psychology (or something similar), the psychology course itself would not be considered
             <em>
              computer science
             </em>
             . If it focused on the human handling and interpretation of data, it might be considered
             <em>
              informatics
             </em>
             .
            </p>

            <p>
             The term “informatics” has different definitions depending on where it is used. In Europe, for instance, computer science is referred to as informatics. In the U.S., however, informatics
is linked with applied computing, or computing in the context of another domain.
            </p>

             School of Informatics and Computing:
