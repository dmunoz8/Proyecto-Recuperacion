       <div class="clearfix" id="main">
        <h1 class="title" id="page-title">
         Exploratory Track in Computing and Informatics
        </h1>
        <!-- #tabs -->
        <div class="tabs">
        </div>
        <!-- EOF: #tabs -->
        <!-- #action links -->
        <!-- EOF: #action links -->
        <div class="region region-content">
         <div class="block block-system clearfix" id="block-system-main">
          <div class="content">
           <div class="ds-1col node node-page view-mode-full clearfix">
            <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden">
             <div class="field-items">
              <div class="field-item even">
               <p>
                The University of Massachusetts Amherst recognizes many of our incoming students would benefit from learning more about the many majors offered on campus that may be a good fit for their academic interests and aspirations. In the Fall of 2015, the University implemented an Exploratory Track program for all students accepted for admission to the University as an undeclared major. The Exploratory Track program assists students with their exploration and ultimate declaration of a major based on their interest, eligibility, and academic proficiency.
               </p>
               <p>
                Undeclared students are placed into an Exploratory Track based on a review of their University admissions application materials. As undeclared majors, Exploratory Track students benefit from working closely with academic advisors to explore potential majors within their college and across the university. Exploratory Track students also participate in academic programming, including a first-year seminar, which is supported by their college.
               </p>
               <h2>
                About the Computing and Informatics Exploratory Track
               </h2>
               <p>
                A select number of students who applied to Computer Science but did not receive direct acceptance will be admitted to the Computing and Informatics Exploratory Track.  These students will explore their interests in a variety of fields within computer science and informatics (the application of computing and information systems).  These fields include computer programming, networking, cyber security, big data, and new fields such as multimedia, learning technologies, digital forensics and health informatics.  Students in this exploratory track will be advised on the appropriate coursework and requirements needed for admission to the Computer Science major, as well as on other majors across campus where they may pursue their interests in computing.
               </p>
               <h2>
                Advising
               </h2>
               <p>
                As an Exploratory Track student, you have access to one-on-one advising that is designed to assist with course selection, major exploration, requirements, and campus resources, as well as general questions about acclimating to college life.
               </p>
               <p>
                The advisor for CICS Exploratory Track Students is Greg Boisseau. To
                <strong>
                 make an advising appointment
                </strong>
                , please call 413-545-4822 or stop by our office (Computer Science Building, Rm. 120).
                <strong>
                </strong>
                Office hours are Monday - Friday, 9:00am - 5:00pm.
               </p>
               <p>
               </p>
