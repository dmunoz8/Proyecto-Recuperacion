        <h1>
         Equality impact assessment - SCIM applicant visit days
        </h1>
        <h2>
         Title of policy
        </h2>
        <p>
         School of Computing, Informatics and Media ¿ applicant visit days.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Key findings of the equality impact assessment
        </h2>
        <p>
         With any event there is the possibility for potential or actual adverse impact, including discrimination. With applicant visit days for potential students, issues may include access to the campus, the timings of the applicant visit days, and offering potential students the chance to see the University without visiting to allow for disability, maternity and so on. It is therefore essential that when planning events, consideration is given to each equality characteristic to ensure that every reasonable precaution has been put in place to avoid such impacts.
        </p>
        <p>
         Further development of virtual tours and other methods of sending information to applicants should they not be able to visit the campus should be undertaken. This will also provide Overseas students the opportunity to access the same type of information as UK based students, as at present it is sometimes difficult for them to obtain visas to visit the University.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Information and data used in the equality impact assessment
        </h2>
        <p>
         Feedback questionnaires from potential students; data analysis from feedback forms and bookings; consultations with Equality Unit and other relevant stakeholders.
        </p>
        <h2>
         Actions taken
        </h2>
        <ul>
         <li>
          Consider the development of virtual tours and further online provision for applicants to ensure everyone has access to data.
         </li>
         <li>
          Review AVD provision during the 2010/11 academic year to collate statistical data on applicants to allow further equality assessment to take place.
          <br/>
         </li>
        </ul>
